General liability insurance covers physical injuries and property damage your business causes to others, while professional liability insurance (also called Errors & Omissions or E&O) covers financial losses caused by your professional advice, services, or mistakes. They protect against completely different types of risk — and many Colorado businesses need both.

Understanding the distinction is critical because carrying the wrong type of coverage can leave you exposed to the exact claims you're most likely to face.

## What General Liability Covers

General liability insurance is the foundation of most business insurance programs. It protects your business when your operations cause:

- **Bodily injury to third parties** — A customer slips on a wet floor in your shop and breaks their wrist
- **Property damage** — Your employee accidentally damages a client's equipment while on a job site
- **Personal and advertising injury** — A competitor claims your ad copied their slogan
- **Products liability** — A product you sell causes injury to a customer
- **Medical payments** — Covers immediate medical costs for someone injured on your premises, regardless of fault

General liability is often called "slip-and-fall" insurance because that's one of the most common claim scenarios. It's about physical harm and tangible damage.

## What Professional Liability (E&O) Covers

Professional liability insurance protects you when your professional services cause a client financial harm. It covers:

- **Negligence or errors** — An accountant makes a tax filing error that costs a client thousands in penalties
- **Omissions** — A consultant fails to include a critical recommendation, leading to a client's financial loss
- **Misrepresentation** — An IT professional overstates the capabilities of a solution they're implementing
- **Missed deadlines** — A designer's late delivery causes a client to miss a product launch
- **Copyright infringement** — A marketing agency unknowingly uses copyrighted material in a campaign
- **Legal defense costs** — Even if the claim is unfounded, E&O covers the cost of defending yourself

Professional liability is about protecting against claims that your work product, advice, or services fell short of expectations and caused measurable financial damage.

## Key Differences at a Glance

**General Liability:**
- Covers physical injuries and property damage
- Triggered by your operations or premises
- Claims involve bodily harm or tangible damage
- Typically costs $30-75/month for small businesses
- Almost every business needs it

**Professional Liability (E&O):**
- Covers financial losses from professional services
- Triggered by your advice, expertise, or work product
- Claims involve monetary damages, not physical harm
- Typically costs $50-150/month depending on profession
- Needed by any business providing professional services or advice

## When You Need Both

Many Colorado businesses need both policies working together. Here's why:

A general contractor needs GL for the physical risks on a job site — a worker drops a tool on someone, scaffolding damages a neighboring property. But they also need professional liability if they provide design advice, project management, or consulting services where an error could cost a client money.

**Businesses that typically need both:**
- IT consultants and MSPs
- Architects and engineers
- Marketing and advertising agencies
- Financial advisors and accountants
- Real estate professionals
- Management consultants
- Healthcare practitioners

## Real-World Examples

**GL claim:** A landscaping company accidentally breaks a client's irrigation system while mowing. General liability covers the repair cost.

**E&O claim:** A financial advisor recommends an investment strategy that doesn't account for the client's risk tolerance, resulting in significant losses. Professional liability covers the claim.

**Both needed:** A web development agency has clients visiting their office (GL for slip-and-fall risk) and delivers code that contains a security vulnerability leading to a data breach at the client's company (E&O for the professional error).

## Cost Comparison

In Colorado, small businesses typically pay:

- **General liability:** $30-75/month ($360-900/year)
- **Professional liability:** $50-150/month ($600-1,800/year)
- **Both combined:** Many carriers offer package pricing that's less than buying separately

Your actual cost depends on your industry, revenue, number of employees, and claims history.

## How to Determine What You Need

Ask yourself these questions:

1. **Do clients or customers visit your location?** You need GL.
2. **Do you provide advice, consulting, or professional services?** You need E&O.
3. **Do your contracts require either type?** Get what's required.
4. **Could an error in your work cause a client financial loss?** You need E&O.
5. **Could someone be physically injured by your operations?** You need GL.

If you answered yes to questions from both categories, you need both policies.
